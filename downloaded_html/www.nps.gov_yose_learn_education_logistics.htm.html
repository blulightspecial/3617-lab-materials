Field Trip Planning and Logistics

Whether you plan to arrange a Parks as Classrooms Ranger-Led Program or want to create a self-guided experience, you will find many of the following tips helpful in order to experience Yosemite as the ultimate outdoor classroom.

Before Your Class Visits Yosemite

Entrance Fee Waiver

Your group may qualify for an entrance fee waiver (check for specific application instructions and contact information). If your group does not qualify, be prepared to pay required fees and possibly make a reservation (if required on the date of your trip).

Camping and Lodging

Yosemite has a wide variety of lodging and camping options, inside as well as outside the park. Please note that it can be very difficult to get lodging or camping reservations, so make a reservation as early as possible. It is up to the school or youth organization to coordinate camping and lodging.

Driving Times and Road Conditions

When calculating driving time, remember that all roads into Yosemite are curvy mountain roads, with relatively low speed limits which may slow bus travel.

  • Directions and maps

  • Driving times

Seasons and Weather

Get a sense of what to expect during your visit; check road conditions and open areas as well as average temperatures during the month of your visit. Weather can change swiftly in the mountains, particularly in the spring, fall, and winter. Layers of clothing and comfortable walking shoes are useful. Check the Yosemite Guide to discover current happenings and activities in the park (you can also find Yosemite Guide issues from previous years to get a general sense of available activities during your visit).

Areas to Visit

See the self-guided trips page for more detailed information about different parts of the park that may be worthwhile for your class to visit.

Food

In Yosemite Valley, Degnan's Kitchen is less than a five-minute walk from the Welcome Center. Yosemite Valley Lodge also has a cafeteria-style food court. If bringing your own lunch, there is a public area (with benches only) in the Yosemite Valley Lodge amphitheater area. A few large bench areas also exist in front of the Yosemite Museum, as well as in front of the Yosemite Exploration Center. These are public areas and cannot be reserved. Please remember to keep close track of all food and trash, and please do not feed wildlife, for your safety and theirs. Picnic areas are also available throughout the park.

Pre-Visit Resources

The park has a number of short, engaging Yosemite Nature Notes videos about different topics like glaciers, granite, giant sequoias and many other topics. Additional videos about other important Yosemite topics can also be viewed from the comfort of your own home or classroom. Information is also available about nature and science and history and culture for student research topics. Finally, invite a ranger into your classroom through distance learning.

Day of Your Field Trip

Bus Parking

Yosemite Valley

All buses must park in the special bus parking area at Yosemite Valley Lodge. There is a bus drop-off area in front of Yosemite Valley Lodge. The bus parking area is about a five-minute walk from the drop-off point. Generally, groups should plan on taking what they need from the bus when unloading.

Mariposa Grove

Reservations are required for all commercial carriers with vehicle capacity of 16 or more arriving to the Mariposa Grove Welcome Plaza. Commercial parking reservations are only available to commercial operators having a commercial use authorization (CUA) from Yosemite National Park, and private, nonprofit bus groups.

Schools planning to visit the Mariposa Grove by bus should email the Educational Fee Waiver Office for parking information and do not need to make advanced reservations online.

Restroom Facilities

When in doubt, ask someone in uniform for the closest restroom facilities. Here are a few for your reference.

Yosemite Valley Lodge

There is a walkway to the right of the front desk. Restrooms are at the end of the corridor on the left. If these restrooms are closed, continue past these restrooms out the door to the amphitheater. Across the amphitheater you will see signs for the Mountain Room. Head into this inside area, and you will see signs to the right for restrooms. There are also limited restrooms located in the Yosemite Valley Lodge Food Court.

Lower Yosemite Fall

This large restroom facility is about a five-minute walk from the Lower Yosemite Fall shuttle stop (#6).

Yosemite Exploration Center and Yosemite Museum

Restrooms are located between the two buildings. This is near shuttle stop #5. If these restrooms are closed, walk five minutes past the Yosemite Exploration Center and Ansel Adams Gallery (toward the Village Store) to Degnan's Kitchen. Restrooms are inside the facility, near the stairs. (Restrooms are also available on both sides of the Village Store.)

Mariposa Grove

Restrooms are available at the Mariposa Grove Welcome Plaza as well as within the grove itself.

Wawona area and Yosemite History Center

Restrooms are located just behind the Wawona Store parking area (across from the Grey Barn).
