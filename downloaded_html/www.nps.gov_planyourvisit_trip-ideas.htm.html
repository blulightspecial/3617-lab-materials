      • Bryce Canyon National Park

        A Typical Summer Visit in 1-3 Hours

        • Type: Active, Relaxed, Indoors, Outdoors, Group Friendly
        • Duration: Half Day
        • Topics: Scenic Views, Animals, Geology, Native American Heritage, Natural Sounds, Night Sky, Ancient Seas, Archeology, Canyons and Canyonlands, Plateaus and Mesas, Fossils and Paleontology, Rock Landscapes and Features, Farming and Agriculture, Freshwater Springs, Astronomy, Trails, Schools and Education, Photography
        • Activities: Scenic Driving, Museum Exhibits, Guided Tours, Front-Country Hiking, Picnicking, Park Film

        If you're a first time visitor, we recommend spending the majority of your time in the Bryce Amphitheater. This iconic area is located along the first three miles of the main road and is home to some of the most popular trails in the park, including Queen's Garden and Navajo Loop. These hikes offer an up-close and intimate look at the highest concentration of hoodoos in the world!

      • Bryce Canyon National Park

        Bryce Amphitheater Hikes

        • Type: Kid Friendly, Active, Relaxed, Educational, Inspirational, Outdoors, Group Friendly
        • Duration: Half Day
        • Topics: Canyons and Canyonlands, Plateaus and Mesas, Geology, Night Sky, Rock Landscapes and Features, Scenic Views, Trails, Wilderness, Astronomy, Photography
        • Activities: Front-Country Hiking, Backcountry Hiking

        The iconic Bryce Canyon Amphitheater is located along the first three miles of the main road, and has four main viewpoints: Bryce, Inspiration, Sunset, and Sunrise Points. Most of the park's day hikes are in this area, originating from these viewpoints. From easy hikes on the rim with views into the amphitheater to more strenuous hikes walking amongst the hoodoos, this area has something for almost everyone. We recommend starting here if you're short on time.

      • Little Rock Central High School National Historic Site

        A Half Day at Central High School NHS

        • Type: Kid Friendly, Relaxed, Urban, Educational, Inspirational, Volunteer, Indoors, Outdoors, Road Trip, Group Friendly
        • Duration: Half Day
        • Topics: African American Heritage, US Army, Presidents, Social Movements, Civil Rights, Urban America, Architecture and Building, Schools and Education, Sculpture, Monuments and Memorials, Scenic Views
        • Activities: Museum Exhibits, Hands-On, Self-Guided Tours - Walking

        Plan ahead and reserve an available ranger-led tour that goes by Little Rock Central High School. View the exhibits at the park's Visitor Center, learn about the Little Rock Nine, watch the park's interpretive film, and explore the other historic buildings and outdoor activities within the park. Earn your Junior Ranger badge by completing activities within the park. Stamp your passport book in our visitor center to capture memories from your visit to our site.
